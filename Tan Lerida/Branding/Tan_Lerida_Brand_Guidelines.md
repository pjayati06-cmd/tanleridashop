**03**

# **Brand Story**

**THE LERIDA LEGACY**

Deep in the ancient caves of Lerida, our ancestors first discovered the transformative power of leather \- how raw hide could become something beautiful, functional, and lasting. Thousands of years later, in the bustling workshops of India, that same spirit of transformation lives on.

Tan Lerida was born from a simple belief: that the things we carry should carry us through life's most meaningful moments. Our craftspeople don't just make leather goods; they create companions for your journey.

Every stitch tells a story. Every piece begins its life in our workshop but finds its purpose in yours, whether that's in a boardroom meeting, on a hiking trail, or in a family kitchen where memories are made.

We're not just honouring the past; we're crafting the future of how we live, work, and connect. Because at Tan Lerida, we believe that when something is made with intention, love, and skill, it doesn't just serve you, it becomes part of who you are.

This is leather. This is life. This is Tan Lerida.

* Has retired the logo phase.

38 to 58\. Corporate gifting that reflects their own taste. Has stopped giving pen sets. Needs the story to be easy to pass on, because the story is part of the gift.

**05**

# **Positioning**

**CATEGORY**

New Indian Heritage Luxury.

*Not affordable luxury. Not aspirational luxury. Honest luxury.*

**ON "AFFORDABLE LUXURY"**

This phrase must not appear in an external copy. It implies compromise and positions the brand relative to a ceiling it doesn't recognise as its own.

Internally the intent is right: Tan Lerida competes on quality with far more expensive international brands, priced to reach the right customer. The external expression of that is not a phrase. It is the product, the lineage, the certifications. Let those make the case.

| *Tan Lerida is the only leather brand that creates entirely at source, from tannery to finished product, rooted in 70+ years of Indian craft knowledge and certified to global standards. For people who buy once and keep forever.* |

**07**

# **Photography**

Not perfection. Truth. The product should look like it has already lived a life, or is about to begin one.

**CORE PRINCIPLES**

| Texture is the hero | Real grain. Real light. No retouching of natural leather texture. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Patina is a feature** | Wear and ageing are assets. Shoot product new, broken-in, and well-loved. |
| **Light is directional** | Warm, natural, raking. No flat studio fill. Side or back-lit for texture depth. |
| **Context, not catalogue** | Products in use or at rest. Worn wood, concrete, stone, linen. No white backgrounds. |
| **People, not models** | When people appear: understated, mid-action, not posing. Using the product. |

**INSTAGRAM FEED**

* 4:5 portrait. Consistent warm colour temperature. High contrast against surface.

* 90% product or detail shots. Lifestyle only when it adds genuine context.

* No text overlays. Copy lives in the caption.

* Grid rhythm: close-detail, full product, environmental. Rotate.

**INSTAGRAM STORIES & REELS**

* Process-forward: grain close-ups, stitching, hardware, tannery clips.

* Short and unhurried. 15 to 30 seconds. No fast cuts.

* No trending audio on product content. Ambient or original only.

**WEBSITE**

| Hero | Large-format, editorial. Directional natural light. No composite backgrounds. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Product shots** | Front, back, profile, open interior, hardware detail. All mandatory. |
| **Scale reference** | At least one shot per product with a hand or wrist for context. |
| **Interior** | Show lining, pockets, construction. Part of the quality story. |

**BY PRODUCT TYPE**

*Garments: jackets, skirts, blazers*

* On-body and off. Capture drape and weight. Substantial, not stiff.

* Detail shots: collar, hem, pocket placement, sleeve lining.

*Goods: bags, wallets, belts*

* Multiple angles are mandatory. Shoot bags with contents where natural.

* Hard light on hardware. It tests quality.

*Accessories*

* Fill the frame with grain, stitching, edge finishing.

* Show scale. Small goods lose their quality story in wide shots.

**POST-PROCESSING**

* Warm tones, never saturated. Lift shadows slightly. Pull highlights back.

* No leather retouching. Show the real material.

* No AI-generated backgrounds. Always real surfaces.

**NEVER SHOOT**

| White background | Removes context. Signals catalogue, not brand. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Lifestyle energy** | Models in motion, aspirational lighting, beauty-campaign styling. |
| **Craft clichés** | Hands-on-leather in golden hour. Artisan staring at work. Product with flowers. |
| **Competing props** | No coffee cups, candles, or clutter. The product is the point. |

**08**

# **Voice & Tone**

**SIX TRAITS**

| Assured | Speaks without hedging. States things as fact. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Rooted** | India is the foundation, not a footnote. Provenance stated plainly. |
| **Understated** | Lets product and process do the talking. No overclaiming. |
| **Craft-obsessed** | Always specific. Names the material, the grade, the place. |
| **Unapologetic** | Doesn't position relative to Western luxury. Needs no permission. |
| **Warm** | Human, not corporate. Second person always: you and we. |

**RULES**

* "You" and "we" always. No distance.

* Every claim anchored to something real: a material, a process, a place.

* One true thing said plainly beats five impressive things said vaguely.

* Short copy: Assured and Craft-obsessed. Long copy: Warm and Rooted.

* Never make the value argument explicitly. Let the product make it.

**REACH FOR THESE**

*you  ·  we  ·  our tannery  ·  full-grain  ·  35 years  ·  70 years  ·  softens  ·  shaped  ·  traceable  ·  worn-in  ·  made  ·  hide to hand  ·  LWG Gold  ·  patina  ·  at source*

**NEVER USE THESE**

*luxury  ·  premium  ·  exclusive  ·  elevate  ·  curated  ·  effortless  ·  sustainable (standalone)  ·  discerning  ·  artisanal  ·  bespoke  ·  opulent*

**BY CHANNEL**

| Instagram Caption | One true thing per post. Can be a single sentence. Lead with craft, close with observation. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Instagram Bio** | Functional. What we are and where we come from. |
| **Website Product** | Specific on material, grade, construction, use. No atmosphere without substance. |
| **Website Heritage** | The Lerida Legacy tone. Can be longer. Must earn its length. |
| **Stories & Reels** | Most conversational. Still never promotional or trend-reactive. |

**09**

# **What to Avoid**

**VISUAL**

| Fast fashion lookbook | Hair blowing, white backgrounds, models mid-leap. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Loud branding** | Oversized logos, visible marks beyond a restrained stamp. |
| **Startup palette** | Millennial pink, sage green, dusty blue, pastel anything. |
| **Processed photography** | Heavy retouching, CGI leather, fake textures. |
| **Craft clichés** | Golden-hour hands-on-leather. Artisan gazing at work. |
| **Heavy black and gold** | The Sabyasachi problem. Use the palette with restraint. |
| **Didot or Bodoni** | Too Dior. Signals aspiration toward someone else's identity. |

**COPY**

| Heritage bragging | "70 years of excellence" stated flatly. Credential without story. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Sustainability boilerplate** | "Committed to sustainable practices." Vague. Indistinguishable from anyone else. |
| **Positioning against the West** | "Rivals European luxury." We don't need that comparison. |
| **Humble startup voice** | "We believe that..." or "Our journey began..." Wrong register. |
| **Performed Indianness** | Hindi words or Sanskrit dropped in to signal heritage. Demonstrate it, don't perform it. |
