**The standard leather goods margin structure:**

Your cost advantage is real — integrated tannery means your leather cost is 30–40% lower than a non-integrated competitor. That gap is your weapon. You have two choices with it:

**Option A — Price-match and pocket the difference.** Sell at market rate, earn 10–15% more margin than competitors. Sustainable, but doesn't build brand value.

**Option B — Price slightly below market and take volume.** Use the cost advantage to win bulk B2B deals that competitors can't profitably match. This is actually your stronger short-term play.

**A few things worth flagging:**

**Don't confuse markup and margin.** A 100% markup on cost gives you 50% gross margin. When people say "we work on 40% margins" in retail, that's usually gross margin — which means they marked up roughly 67% on cost. Make sure you're comparing apples when talking to buyers.

**B2B export pricing needs to account for Incoterms.** FOB vs CIF vs DDP changes your effective margin by 5–15%. Know exactly what you're pricing before quoting.

**LWG Gold is real pricing leverage.** Global brands with ESG mandates — especially European and UK buyers — will pay a 10–20% premium for certified supply. Your current B2B pricing shouldn't give that away for free. Make it a line item in your pitch.

**For Tan Lerida specifically** — if you're building a consumer brand on top of SPC's manufacturing, your online D2C margin should be targeting 65–70% gross margin (not markup), which means pricing at roughly 3–3.5x your cost. Anything less makes it hard to fund marketing and brand-building.

 Sure. Let's use a wallet.

---

**You make a wallet. It costs you ₹100 to make.**

---

**MARKUP** is what you ADD ON TOP of your cost.

100% markup = you add ₹100 on top.
So you sell it for **₹200**.

Simple. You doubled the price. That's 100% markup.

---

**MARGIN** is what you KEEP out of what you charged.

You sold it for ₹200. You kept ₹100 profit.
₹100 out of ₹200 = **50%**.

So you kept half of what the customer paid you.

---

**Why they're different:**

Markup asks: *"How much did I add on top of my cost?"*
Margin asks: *"How much of the selling price is actually mine?"*

Same transaction. Same wallet. Same numbers. Two different ways of describing it.

---

**Where people get confused:**

A buyer tells you: *"We work on 40% margins."*

You think — okay, I'll mark up 40% on my cost.

Your cost is ₹100. You charge ₹140. You think you're fine.

But they meant 40% of the **selling price** is their profit. So they're actually keeping ₹56 out of every ₹140 sale — and their cost to them is only ₹84.

That means **they expect to buy from you at ₹84, not ₹140.** You just accidentally gave away ₹16 because you were speaking different languages.

---

**The one-line rule:**

Markup is calculated on **cost** (what you paid).
Margin is calculated on **price** (what you charged).

Same profit. Different denominators. Always clarify which one someone means before you agree to anything.

## **What is Enterprise Account Management?**

Enterprise Account Management is the strategic management of relationships with large, complex organizations (typically companies with 1,000+ employees or multi-million dollar revenues). It goes far beyond basic client servicing - it's about becoming a strategic business partner.

## **Key Characteristics:**

### **Scale & Complexity:**
- **Large budgets** - often £100K+ to multi-million pound contracts
- **Multiple stakeholders** - you're dealing with CMOs, procurement, IT, operations teams
- **Complex decision-making processes** - 6-12 month sales cycles are normal
- **Multi-location deployments** - campaigns across hundreds or thousands of locations

### **Strategic vs. Transactional:**
- **Long-term partnerships** (3-5+ year relationships)
- **Business consultation** rather than order-taking
- **Revenue growth planning** and expansion opportunities
- **Risk management** and contingency planning

## **Day-to-Day Responsibilities:**

### **Relationship Management:**
- **Executive-level meetings** with C-suite stakeholders

### **Account Development:**

- **Contract negotiations** and renewal management
