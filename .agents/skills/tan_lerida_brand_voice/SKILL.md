# Tan Lerida Brand Voice Skill

Use this skill when writing copy, designing UI layouts, or planning campaigns for Tan Lerida.

## 1. Brand Identity & Positioning

*   **Category**: New Indian Heritage Luxury. Not affordable, not aspirational—honest luxury.
*   **Taglines**:
    *   *Made for Living*: Customer-facing everyday copy, Instagram, packaging.
    *   *Luxury is Handmade*: Brand campaigns, credentials, brand story.
*   **Philosophy**: Celebrating the art of living through craft that tells your story. Focus on hobbies, experiences, and life companions.

## 2. Customer Persona Focus

Every visual and textual decision should be tested against:
*   **The Earned Life**: Age 32-48. Founders, consultants, architects, surgeons. Built wealth through competence. Wants high-quality, long-lasting companions. Done with logo-seeking.
*   **The Global Indian**: NRI/PIO, 28-45. Seeks genuine Indian craft, not tourism-oriented or mass export styles. Proud of Indian tannery heritage.
*   **The Gifter**: Corporate/wedding premium gifting where the story is part of the gift.

## 3. Verbal Guidelines & Vocabulary

### Reach For (Approved Terms)
`you`, `we`, `our tannery`, `full-grain`, `35 years`, `70 years`, `softens`, `shaped`, `traceable`, `worn-in`, `made`, `hide to hand`, `LWG Gold`, `patina`, `at source`, `Made for Living`, `Luxury is Handmade`.

### Never Use (Prohibited Terms)
`luxury` (unless paired in taglines), `premium`, `exclusive`, `elevate`, `curated`, `effortless`, `sustainable` (use specific certifications like LWG Gold instead), `discerning`, `artisanal`, `bespoke`, `opulent`.

### General Tone Rules
*   Speak without hedging (Assured).
*   No standard divider lines or startups palettes (Sage, pastel pink).
*   One true thing said plainly beats five impressive things said vaguely.
*   Always address the audience as "you" and refer to the brand as "we".
